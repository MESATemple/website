Who we are

The Pennsylvania MESA is part of the 10-state MESA USA network (www.mesausa.org) which serves 50,000 students in grades K-12 including community college students. PA MESA is licensed by the University of California to Temple University which sponsors the initiative at the College of Engineering in the Department of STEM Education, Outreach and Research. Dedicated staff, engineering students and volunteers from a wide variety of science, technology, engineering and math backgrounds guide pre-college students through programs that increase knowledge about careers that support the world's critical infrastructure - access to clean water, nutritious food, healthy living and renewable energy.

What we do

The Pennsylvania MESA is part of the 10-state MESA USA network (www.mesausa.org) which serves 50,000 students in grades K-12 including community college students. PA MESA is licensed by the University of California to Temple University which sponsors the initiative at the College of Engineering in the Department of STEM Education, Outreach and Research. Dedicated staff, engineering students and volunteers from a wide variety of science, technology, engineering and math backgrounds guide pre-college students through programs that increase knowledge about careers that support the world's critical infrastructure - access to clean water, nutritious food, healthy living and renewable energy.

Enroll in year round Saturday Academies in engineering and computer science led by Temple adjunct faculty

Register for summer camp for middle and high school students

Host after-school clubs that rely on creativity and teamwork to solve humanity's "grand challenges"

Use the published MESA curriculum to foster teacher co-curricular planning, collaboration and confidence

The annual kick off event is in October of each year. Schools can sponsor one team, or an intramural league that works on applying science, math, literacy, creativity and design thinking for immediate or future challenges. Participating in regional or national MESA competitions is strongly encouraged as a goal that sets high expectations. In the past five years, PA MESA has served nearly 4,000 students from the greater Philadelphia area through workshops, outreach and dedicated programs on campus and after school. In addition, we've helped two dozen undergraduate engineering students graduate by creating work-study jobs serving as MESA mentors!

Our Mission

Secure public and private resources to advance competitive academics to increase the number of engineers, scientists, mathematicians, and related professionals at technical and management levels, and encourage and assist minorities and females in achieving success in these fields

Our Vision

Contribute to a competitive global workforce by increasing the yield and diversity of STEM graduates in Pennsylvania who follow various education and training pathways.

Core Activities

PA MESA offers students college readiness, career exploration and skill building through:

  • Monthly engineering and computer science workshops at Engineering
  • Undergraduate student ambassadors to assist with MESA team projects
  • Access to STEM experts who provide tours, mentoring and volunteers
  • Tutoring in STEM disciplines

Teachers can develop strong skills infusing project based learning using MESA curriculum modules. Teacher PD is offered 3-times per year and eligible for up to 8 hours of Act48 credits.

A Message From our Director

“My doctorate is in educational psychology with a focus on how culture impacts cognition and STEM learning for minority children. My inspirational heroes are Asa Hilliard III, Gloria Ladson, Kurt Lewin, Reginald Lewis, Amos Wilson, Albert Bandura, Michelle Obama and my children. In fact, I didn't start my professional career interested in STEM education, I started in corporate America to make some money! But, I became aware of the significant lack of opportunities in engineering and science when I couldn't find any early engineering programs for my younger sons. To solve the problem, I helped organize a group of community advocates (the Urban STEM Strategy Group) and we hosted public hearings with the PA House Education committee that led to more attention on STEM access across Pennsylvania. I joined the Temple Intergenerational Center and oversaw infusing STEM into the Philadelphia School District’s GEARUP initiative. I was then named the founding director and leader of a STEM department at Temple Engineering that’s become award winning and impactful for children, including my own. (One son graduated in 2017 and is now an electrical engineer, the other graduates in 2018 with a degree in mechanical engineering technology). Our teams from this region have placed in nationals, won contests and know the value of competing!! That’s why I’m completely convinced this nation can and must produce talented children from every background - and continue to invest across socioeconomic boundaries since it’s clear poor does not equal “stupid”. I hope you’ll be inspired and help us create clear pathways into a world that values diversity among workers, innovators, leaders and problem solvers. It's about more than any particular program, it's a movement for access, inclusion and high quality and we hope you'll join us.”

Dr. Jamie Bracey has led engagement activities at Temple University for 10 years and has serves on local, state, national and international boards to advance STEM access, inclusion, equity and parity for underrepresented (minority) children in STEM.
